About Me

I'm a software engineer with roots in electronics — I studied at Sabanci University in Istanbul, then completed an IoT & Machine Intelligence graduate program at Sheridan College in Toronto. That combination gives me an unusual depth: I'm as comfortable designing firmware in C as I am building cloud microservices in Java or training neural networks in Python.

At Electric Mind, I led generative AI initiatives, built full-stack products, and architected data pipelines. Before that I wrote firmware for WiFi extenders at Airties and optimised data infrastructure as a remote data scientist at Turing.

Outside work I study markets, read voraciously, and build side projects that live at the intersection of software and the physical world. I believe technology should create genuine value — for people and for the planet.
